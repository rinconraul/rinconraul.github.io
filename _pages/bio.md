I am an incoming Assistant Professor in the Department of Civil, Environmental,
and Architectural Engineering at the **University of Colorado Boulder**, joining
in Fall 2026, where I am building the **Rincon Research Group** to develop
computational and probabilistic frameworks that model, design, and monitor
socio-infrastructure-environment systems so that urban infrastructure performs
safely, resiliently, and equitably now and into the future.

My research argues for a shift away from conventional cause-and-effect reasoning,
where resilience is limited to a single hazard-performance-recovery sequence.
Instead, I pursue *systemic approaches* that capture the complex, adaptive, and
multiscale interactions between societies, infrastructure systems, and
environmental stressors. This means developing algorithmic methods and statistical
tools that can simulate, measure, learn from, and inform decisions about
infrastructure under progressive deterioration, natural hazards, and climate
change, simultaneously and across scales.

A unifying thread is the question: *how do modeling choices made at the component
level propagate into the decisions that shape community resilience?* My research
formalizes this through multiscale fragility and network models,
bias-quantification algorithms, attributed graph representations of
socio-infrastructure interactions, and active-learning frameworks for post-event
damage assessment. I am motivated by a commitment to ensuring that the tools we
build for resilience assessment are objective, transparent, and do not
inadvertently reinforce preexisting social inequities.

Prior to joining CU Boulder, I was a **Postdoctoral Associate at Rice University**
working with [Dr. Jamie Ellen Padgett](https://padgett.rice.edu/) and
[Dr. Mauricio Sanchez-Silva](https://riskandreliability.uniandes.edu.co/miembros/profesor-mauricio-sanchez-silva/)
(Universidad de los Andes, Colombia). I received my Ph.D. from Rice University in
July 2025. Before my doctoral work, I spent three years as a full-time lecturer
and researcher at Universidad de los Andes in Bogotá, contributing to
national-scale projects on seismic risk assessment of schools, bridges, and
critical infrastructure.
