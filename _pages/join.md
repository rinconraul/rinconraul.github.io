## Joining the Rincon Research Group at CU Boulder

I will join the Department of Civil, Environmental, and Architectural Engineering
at the **University of Colorado Boulder** as an Assistant Professor in **Fall 2026**,
and I am building a research group focused on resilience modeling, computational
methods for infrastructure systems, and equitable engineering decision-making.

---

### What we work on

The Rincon Research Group develops algorithms, statistical tools, and probabilistic frameworks
for modeling, designing, and monitoring civil infrastructure systems exposed to
natural hazards, deterioration, and climate stressors — with a commitment to making
those tools accurate, transparent, and equitable.

Our work spans structural fragility modeling, infrastructure network analysis,
machine learning for resilience assessment, graph-theoretic representations of
socio-infrastructure systems, and the relationship between modeling choices and
social outcomes.

If you are drawn to problems at the intersection of **data, uncertainty,
computation, and societal impact** in engineered systems, this may be the right
group for you.

---

### What I look for in PhD students

Strong candidates typically have some combination of:

- A background in civil/structural engineering, applied mechanics, systems
  engineering, or a closely related quantitative field
- Comfort with — or genuine interest in developing — probabilistic and
  computational skills (probability, statistics, Python or MATLAB)
- Intellectual curiosity about how engineering models connect to real decisions
  about infrastructure and communities
- Prior research experience (undergraduate thesis, co-authorship, lab work)
  is valued but not required

I particularly welcome applications from students with backgrounds underrepresented
in engineering research, including students from Latin America and other
international contexts.

---

### What working in the group looks like

My approach to mentoring is shaped by years of guiding undergraduate researchers
(I advised 17 senior research projects at Universidad de los Andes) and by my own
experience being trained across multiple countries and research cultures.

In the Rincon Research Group, you can expect:

- **Substantive, regular mentoring** — weekly one-on-ones, not a hands-off model
- **Exposure to the full research lifecycle** — from problem formulation through
  writing, presentation, and community engagement
- **Professional skills development** — communication, research planning, and
  academic writing, because these matter whether you stay in academia or go to industry
- **Honest career conversations** — your goals and trajectory matter, and I will
  help you navigate them whatever direction they take
- **A collegial, intellectually serious environment** where new ideas are welcomed
  and existing ones are questioned respectfully

---

### PhD funding and CU Boulder context

PhD students in CEAE at CU Boulder are typically supported through research
assistantships covering tuition and a competitive stipend. I expect to support
PhD students through my research program.

CU Boulder's CEAE department has particular strength in natural hazards, structural
engineering, and sustainable infrastructure. Boulder's broader research ecosystem
includes NIST's Community Resilience Program, NCAR, and strong connections to
federal agencies in the Rocky Mountain region — all directly relevant to this
lab's research agenda.
